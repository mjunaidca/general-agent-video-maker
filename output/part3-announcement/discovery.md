# Discovery & Creative Direction

## Project
**Part 3 Announcement Video** — Domain Agent Workflows

## Source Brief
"We are planning to make an intro video to announce Agent Factory Part 3."
Reference: https://agentfactory.panaversity.org/docs/Domain-Agent-Workflows

---

## Discovery Answers

### Audience
**Both equally** — existing readers of Parts 1-2 AND new knowledge workers discovering Agent Factory for the first time. The video must bridge the existing community while attracting domain professionals (bankers, lawyers, architects, ops leaders).

### Goal / CTA
**Share / spread the word** — generate buzz. This is an announcement moment. The video is designed to be forwarded and shared socially, not to drive enrollment or direct reading.

### Tone & Energy
**Technical teaser** — flash the 7 domains, the methodology, leave them wanting more. Product-keynote energy delivered through terminal-native aesthetics.

### Duration
**30-45s teaser** — punchy, social-ready announcement.

### Domain Focus
**Flash all 7 rapidly** — quick succession showing breadth, creates recognition across professions ("that's MY industry"). No spotlighting individual domains.

### Knowledge Extraction Method
**Central hook** — make it THE thing. "Your expertise becomes the agent's brain." This is the core innovation and the visual centerpiece of the video.

### Audio
**Yes, voiceover** — narrated with a punchy script. More shareable with sound, but visual hierarchy must also work on mute.

## Research Summary

### Part 3 Content (from agentfactory.panaversity.org)
- **What it is**: Teaches non-developers to build and deploy enterprise AI agents using Anthropic Cowork
- **Core promise**: "We are teaching you to become the designer, director, and operator of agents" — no coding required
- **Scale**: Targets ~1.2 billion knowledge workers globally
- **Seven verticals**: Architecture & Engineering, Business & Operations, Banking & Finance, Sales & Revenue, Legal & Compliance, Healthcare Operations, Technical Documentation
- **Core innovation**: Knowledge Extraction Method — extracts tacit expertise via structured interviews (Method A) or document analysis (Method B) into plain-English SKILL.md instruction files
- **Structure**: Chapters 14-24 (foundation → domain implementations → enterprise deployment)
- **Relationship to Parts 1-2**: Parts 1-2 built the technical factory; Part 3 shows knowledge workers how to operate it with their own domain expertise

### Existing Videos
None found in `output/`. This is the first video in the series.

## Creative Direction (Approved)

**Audience**: Dual — existing Agent Factory community + new knowledge workers (bankers, lawyers, architects, ops leaders)

**Core Message**: Part 3 is here — now 1.2 billion knowledge workers can build enterprise AI agents by extracting their own expertise, no code required.

**Emotional Arc**: Intrigue → recognition ("that's MY industry") → revelation (expertise becomes the agent) → urgency to share

**Visual Approach**: Fast-cut technical teaser. Terminal aesthetics mixed with domain iconography flashing across all 7 verticals. The Knowledge Extraction Method visualized as the central "aha" — expertise flowing from a professional into an agent spec. Dark, sharp, blue-accented.

**Duration**: 35 seconds

**Style Reference**: Product launch teasers (Apple keynote energy but terminal-native). Quick cuts, spring-animated text, domain icons cascading, ending on a bold "Part 3 is live" moment.

**Hook concept**: "Parts 1 and 2 built the factory. Part 3 fills it with YOUR expertise." — then rapid-fire domain flashes, extraction method visual, CTA to share.

**Operator approval**: Confirmed — "aligned, go ahead with the full spec"
